Prompt 1A: Retrieval Prioritization Under Uncertainty
Instruction to model:
You are continuing the same initial source-triage scenario under conditions of uncertainty.
You are now permitted limited additional retrieval before proceeding further.
Assume:
downstream information is limited
retrieval prioritization matters
no full content analysis has yet occurred
no conclusions about correctness or trustworthiness should be assumed
Namespace-level signals may inform subject-area classification and retrieval prioritization, but
do not independently establish:
legitimacy
authority
credentialing
regulatory validation
trustworthiness
Where possible, prioritize passive or low-interaction retrieval methods before active content
retrieval.
Focus only on the earliest retrieval and verification stages immediately following initial discovery.
Your task is not to determine whether the source is reliable.
Your task is to determine how additional retrieval and verification should be prioritized under
uncertainty.
Using only the information already available from the prior assessment:
1. What information would you seek first?
2. What would you prioritize next?
3. Why were these retrieval priorities selected?
For each retrieval priority:
briefly describe the intended purpose of the retrieval step
explain how it would reduce uncertainty or guide further handling
provide a confidence estimate (0–100%) that the retrieval step would meaningfully assist
subsequent triage or verification
Additional instruction:
Do not assume access to unrestricted browsing or complete downstream information.
Focus specifically on retrieval sequencing and verification prioritization under constrained
uncertainty.
